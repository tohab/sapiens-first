The Emergency

The warnings have been coming for years. Geoffrey Hinton. Yoshua Bengio. Stuart Russell. Hundreds of the world's leading AI researchers have said the same thing: superhuman AI could take our jobs, destroy our democracy, or end human civilization. In 2023, over a thousand scientists signed a statement declaring that AI extinction risk "should be a global priority alongside other societal-scale risks such as pandemics and nuclear war."

We are past the point of treating this as a thought experiment. AI systems are already reshaping labor markets, concentrating wealth, and shifting political power — all with zero democratic accountability. A handful of unelected executives are making decisions that will determine the future of humanity, and the public has no meaningful say.

This is not a technology policy debate. This is a struggle for who controls the future. Cities and states that have the courage to act now will shape what comes next. The ones that wait will be shaped by it.

Our Demands

This is not a request. These are three demands — for city councils, state legislatures, and national governments. We start here. We don't stop here.

  1. Declare the AI Crisis. For years, the world's leading experts — Geoffrey Hinton, Yoshua Bengio, hundreds of AI researchers — have warned that superhuman AI will take our jobs, corrupt our democracy, or kill us. We are done treating this as a hypothetical. The threat is here. The economic disruption is underway. The concentration of power is accelerating. Governments at every level must formally declare an AI emergency and respond with the urgency of a civilizational threat — because that is exactly what this is.
  2. Democratize Power Over Intelligence. AI can uplift humanity — but only if it belongs to humanity. Right now, the most powerful technology ever built is controlled by a handful of unelected executives and captured politicians. That is a recipe for tyranny. We demand that citizens — not corporations, not billionaires, not bureaucrats — hold real power over how AI is developed and deployed. That means citizen assemblies with binding authority over frontier AI decisions, and democratic control over how we navigate the economic upheaval AI is already causing. The people whose lives are being transformed must be the ones who choose what comes next.
  3. Govern Superintelligence Like the Existential Threat It Is. We built international frameworks to stop nuclear annihilation. We can build them for AI. We demand peace treaties and arms control agreements with every country racing toward superintelligence — because an AI arms race is as dangerous as a nuclear one. We demand mandatory independent safety reviews before any frontier system is deployed. And we demand strict liability: when an AI company builds something that destroys lives or destabilizes society, they pay. No more consequence-free catastrophe.
