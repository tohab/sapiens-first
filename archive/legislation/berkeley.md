# The People's AI Resolution — Berkeley

**A Resolution of the City Council of Berkeley, California, Declaring an Artificial Intelligence Emergency and Urging the California State Legislature and the United States Congress to Enact Comprehensive Legislation to Protect the People from the Threats of Unaccountable Artificial Intelligence**

*Status: Draft · 2026*

---

WHEREAS, leading artificial intelligence researchers — including Turing Award winners Geoffrey Hinton and Yoshua Bengio, and more than a thousand scientists who signed the 2023 Statement on AI Risk — have warned that superhuman AI poses risks to human civilization comparable to nuclear war and pandemics, and that these risks can no longer be ignored; and

WHEREAS, AI-driven automation is already displacing workers across industries, with economists projecting the exposure of hundreds of millions of jobs globally to significant displacement in the coming decade, with disproportionate harm falling on working-class and middle-income people who have had no say in the decisions driving this transformation; and

WHEREAS, the productivity and economic gains produced by artificial intelligence are accruing almost entirely to the shareholders of a small number of technology corporations, rather than to the workers, communities, and public institutions whose data, labor, and publicly funded research made these systems possible; and

WHEREAS, the development of frontier AI systems is being driven by an unchecked arms race among a handful of private companies and national governments, with no binding international agreements to prevent catastrophic outcomes — a situation directly analogous to the nuclear arms race of the twentieth century, which required international treaties and arms control agreements to bring under democratic control; and

WHEREAS, the University of California, Berkeley — home to the Center for Human-Compatible Artificial Intelligence, the Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Lab, and the Machine Intelligence Research Institute — is one of the world's foremost centers of both AI capability and AI safety research, and the Berkeley community bears a special responsibility to sound the alarm and demand action; and

WHEREAS, Berkeley has a historic tradition of acting on existential threats before larger institutions are willing to do so — from the Free Speech Movement to sanctuary city status to the Nuclear Free Berkeley Act to the Climate Emergency Declaration — and that tradition demands we act again; and

WHEREAS, decisions about how artificial intelligence reshapes the economy, democracy, and the conditions of human life are currently being made by a small number of unelected corporate executives without any meaningful public input, democratic oversight, or accountability; and

WHEREAS, the concentration of AI power in private hands — absent democratic control — poses a direct threat to the sovereignty of the people and the integrity of democratic institutions; and

WHEREAS, no government body currently has the authority, independence, or resources to meaningfully audit, regulate, or hold accountable the frontier AI systems being deployed at scale; now, therefore, be it

RESOLVED, That the City Council of the City of Berkeley hereby declares an Artificial Intelligence Emergency, recognizing that the development of superhuman artificial intelligence poses an existential threat to the livelihoods, freedoms, and lives of the people of Berkeley, California, and humanity as a whole; and be it further

RESOLVED, That the City Council urges the California State Legislature and the United States Congress to establish citizen assemblies with binding authority over frontier AI development — composed of ordinary people, not industry insiders — empowered to set the terms under which the most powerful AI systems are built, deployed, and controlled, and to direct the democratic management of the economic transition that AI is already underway; and be it further

RESOLVED, That the City Council urges the United States federal government to pursue international peace treaties and arms control agreements with all nations developing frontier AI systems — modeled on nuclear non-proliferation frameworks — to prevent a catastrophic AI arms race, including mutual commitments to halt the development of AI systems that could undermine human control or threaten human survival; and be it further

RESOLVED, That the City Council urges the California State Legislature to establish mandatory independent safety reviews for all frontier AI systems before deployment, with the power to halt deployment of any system that poses unacceptable risks to the public — and to impose strict liability on any company whose AI systems cause material harm to individuals, communities, or democratic institutions; and be it further

RESOLVED, That the City Clerk is directed to transmit a certified copy of this resolution to the Governor of California, the President pro Tempore of the California State Senate, the Speaker of the California State Assembly, the President of the United States, the United States Senators representing California, and the Representatives of each Congressional district that includes any part of the City of Berkeley.
