Here's 3 different models for having an impact.

### 1:1 Growth

Imagine y is the impact you create, and x is the amount of staff time you have.

y = x

We call this 1:1 growth. x goes up, and our impact, y, goes up as well.

### Linear Growth

Imagine that each staff person coordinates A volunteers.

Then, we have:

y = Ax

Linear growth.

In political organizing, this is called the "snowflake model". It was the innovation of Obama's campaign in 2008.

### Exponential Growth

Imagine instead that every volunteer coordinates A more volunteers.

Then, we have:

y = A^x

This is exponential growth. Each additional unit of staff time doesn't just add a constant number of volunteers — it multiplies the entire network.

This is the model Sapiens First is building toward. Not just a staff team coordinating volunteers, but a network where every participant brings in more participants, and every node in the network has real ownership over the mission.

The difference between linear and exponential isn't just about scale. It's about what kind of organization you are.
