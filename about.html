AI must be accountable to the people it affects.

The Guardrail Project is a civic movement dedicated to the democratic governance of artificial intelligence — organized city by city, state by state, building toward federal action.

We are modeled on the movements that actually changed American law — from Mothers Against Drunk Driving to the fight for marriage equality — because grassroots political power is the only force that reliably turns public consensus into legislation.

Who We Are

The Guardrail Project is a civic movement dedicated to the democratic governance of artificial intelligence. We believe that AI — like every powerful technology before it — must be accountable to the people it affects.

We organize ordinary Americans to demand political change: starting in their cities, scaling to their states, and building toward federal action.

We advocate for freedom, democracy, and justice in the age of AI.

Our Political Agenda

We fight for three principles, and under each, a set of rights we believe every American deserves.

Freedom

The Right to Grow Up Free

Every child deserves to grow up free — free to be curious, to play, to dream — without AI systems engineered to capture their attention and exploit their development. Children deserve technology that serves their flourishing, not their addiction.

The Right to Privacy

Every American deserves full ownership of their digital life — their face, their voice, their movements, their messages. We demand the right to know what is collected, to withhold consent, and to be made whole when our information is taken from us.

The Right to Conscience

The people who build AI must be free to tell the truth about it. Workers on the inside see the dangers most clearly, and they deserve the protection to say so. No one should have to choose between their conscience and their livelihood.

Democracy

The Right to a Future

We demand democratic oversight of any AI capable of catastrophic, irreversible harm — protections like those we built for nuclear power and financial markets — that make AI answerable not to shareholders, but to the American people.

The Right to Liberty

AI power must be distributed, accountable, and answerable to the American people. What this country builds must remain under this country's control — defended from foreign adversaries and from unchecked corporate consolidation alike. This power belongs to the public.

The Right to Free Elections

Our elections must be free from AI manipulation. Every American has the right to know when what they see, hear, or read was made by a machine. Our democracy depends on our ability to tell truth from fabrication — and we demand that right be protected.

Justice

The Right to Legal Action

Legal accountability must follow AI wherever it causes harm. Every American harmed by these systems deserves recourse, compensation, and justice.

The Right to Create

Artists, writers, musicians, coders, and creators must own what they make — and must be compensated when their work is used to train AI systems. The creative economy is not raw material to be extracted.

The Right to Inheritance

The gains of AI must be returned to the people whose labor made them possible. The prosperity AI creates belongs not to a handful of companies, but to the workers and communities who built the foundation it stands on.

Our Origins

The Guardrail Project was founded in the recognition that the most important technology of our era is being deployed with almost no democratic oversight — and that the political infrastructure to change that simply didn't exist.

By the mid-2020s, AI had moved from research labs into the fabric of daily life. It was shaping what children see, how workers are hired and fired, how criminal sentences are determined, and how elections are influenced. And yet, there was no federal AI safety legislation of substance. Industry self-regulation had proven insufficient. Academic and policy organizations were producing valuable research — but research alone was not producing law.

What was missing was a political movement.

The founders of the Guardrail Project studied how major reforms actually happen in America — not through white papers and working groups alone, but through organized citizens demanding action from their elected officials. MADD didn't reduce drunk driving deaths by publishing research. They reduced them by putting political pressure on legislators, city by city and state by state, until the laws changed.

The Guardrail Project was built on that model. We began in Berkeley, California — a city with a long tradition of civic action and a City Council willing to lead. From there, we aim to build a network of local campaigns, coalitions, and proven policy models that can scale across the country.

We are early. The movement is forming. And we believe that the decisions made in this political window — right now, while the technology is still taking shape — will determine whether AI becomes a tool of liberation or a tool of extraction for generations to come.
