# The People's AI Resolution — Berkeley

**A Resolution of the City Council of Berkeley, California, Establishing the People's AI Resolution and Urging the California State Legislature to Enact Comprehensive Artificial Intelligence Accountability Legislation**

*Status: Draft · 2026*

---

WHEREAS, artificial intelligence systems are rapidly transforming the economy, labor markets, and public life in California and across the nation; and

WHEREAS, leading economists and technologists project that AI-driven automation may expose hundreds of millions of jobs globally to significant displacement, with disproportionate impacts on working-class and middle-income Californians; and

WHEREAS, the productivity gains arising from AI are currently accruing primarily to technology companies and their shareholders, rather than to the workers, communities, and public institutions whose data, labor, and publicly funded research have contributed to these systems; and

WHEREAS, in 1964, students at the University of California, Berkeley launched the [Free Speech Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_Speech_Movement), which transformed the national conversation about civil liberties and demonstrated that principled civic action originating in Berkeley can reshape policy across the country; and

WHEREAS, in 1971, Berkeley became the first city in the United States to declare itself a [sanctuary city](https://thebolditalic.com/how-berkeley-became-americas-first-sanctuary-city-520bc6a836f1), through Resolution #44,784, offering refuge to Navy sailors opposed to the Vietnam War — language and philosophy later borrowed by policymakers nationwide to protect refugees and immigrants; and

WHEREAS, in 1986, Berkeley voters passed the [Nuclear Free Berkeley Act](https://www.nuclearban.us/nuclear-free-berkeley-act/), establishing the city as a nuclear-free zone and demonstrating that a city can take a principled stand against the power of entrenched industrial and technological interests — and that local action can seed national movements; and

WHEREAS, in 2018, the Berkeley City Council adopted a [Climate Emergency Declaration](https://gofossilfree.org/press-release/berkeley-fossil-free-rise-june/) and a Fossil-Free Resolution committing the city to 100% renewable energy by 2030, establishing a precedent of treating technological and industrial transition as a matter of democratic accountability and public welfare; and

WHEREAS, each of these actions reflected Berkeley's conviction that cities bear a responsibility to name emerging threats to human welfare before larger institutions are willing to act — and that Berkeley has done so, repeatedly, to the ultimate benefit of people far beyond its borders; and

WHEREAS, the University of California, Berkeley, is one of the world's foremost AI research institutions, home to the [Center for Human-Compatible Artificial Intelligence](https://humancompatible.ai) and the [Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research Lab](https://bair.berkeley.edu); and Berkeley is also home to the [Machine Intelligence Research Institute](https://intelligence.org), an independent organization dedicated specifically to ensuring that advanced AI does not pose an existential threat to humanity — making this city a global center of gravity for both AI capability and AI safety research; and

WHEREAS, UC Berkeley Professor Stuart Russell and more than one thousand researchers and scientists signed a statement in 2023 declaring that "[mitigating the risk of extinction from AI should be a global priority alongside other societal-scale risks such as pandemics and nuclear war](https://www.safe.ai/statement-on-ai-risk)" — reflecting a broad scientific consensus that the current trajectory of AI development poses risks not merely to livelihoods but to human civilization itself; and

WHEREAS, New York Assemblyman and Congressional candidate [Alex Bores](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/20/alex-bores-ai-dividend-plan-wealth) — a former AI researcher and co-author of New York's frontier AI safety law — unveiled an AI Dividend plan in April 2026, proposing that if AI dramatically increases productivity and concentrates wealth, the American people have a stake in those gains, to be funded by taxes on AI use and equity stakes in AI companies; and

WHEREAS, OpenAI itself, in its April 2026 economic policy blueprint [*Industrial Policy for the Intelligence Age*](https://openai.com/global-affairs/openais-economic-blueprint/), proposed a national public wealth fund modeled on Alaska's Permanent Fund to distribute AI-driven gains directly to American citizens, and acknowledged that AI-driven automation may shrink payroll tax revenues and threaten Social Security, Medicaid, and other public programs — underscoring the urgency of proactive legislative action; and

WHEREAS, this convergence of proposals across the political and industry spectrum — from progressive legislators to AI companies themselves — reflects a growing consensus that AI's economic gains must be broadly shared, and that unaccountable AI poses risks requiring urgent governmental response; and

WHEREAS, California is home to many of the world's leading AI companies and research institutions, and the California State Legislature has both the authority and the obligation to protect Californians from the harms of unaccountable AI systems while ensuring the gains of this technology are shared broadly; now, therefore, be it

RESOLVED, That the City Council of the City of Berkeley urges the California State Legislature to enact policies ensuring that the gains of AI-driven economic growth are broadly shared — particularly with workers and communities experiencing displacement from automation — rather than concentrated among the shareholders of technology companies; and be it further

RESOLVED, That the City Council urges the California State Legislature to establish robust accountability standards for AI developers and deployers, ensuring that individuals and communities harmed by AI systems have meaningful and enforceable recourse; and be it further

RESOLVED, That the City Council urges the California State Legislature to create strong democratic oversight of AI — including transparency requirements, independent auditing authority, and meaningful civil society participation in governance — so that the technology shaping Californians' lives is ultimately accountable to them; and be it further

RESOLVED, That the City Clerk is directed to transmit a certified copy of this resolution to the Governor of California, the President pro Tempore of the California State Senate, the Speaker of the California State Assembly, and the Mayor and City Council of each city in Alameda County.
