Berkeley
AI Accountability
Resolution

Berkeley has led on free speech, environmental protection, and civil rights. Now we can lead again — urging California to set the standard for how AI works for all of us.

Why Berkeley

Berkeley has always been where California's future takes shape first. From the Free Speech Movement to landmark environmental protections to sanctuary city status, our city has a tradition of acting on principle before the rest of the state catches up. AI accountability is the next chapter in that tradition.

The University of California, Berkeley, is one of the world's foremost AI research institutions. Our community has seen this technology up close — its promise and its risks — which gives Berkeley both a special credibility and a special responsibility to lead.

A resolution passed here doesn't stay here. It becomes a model for every other city in California, and a signal to Sacramento that the public is ready for action. This is how statewide movements begin.

What We're Asking

This resolution urges the California State Legislature to act on three fronts.

01
AI Dividend
If AI displaces Californians at scale, the productivity gains should be shared broadly. The Legislature should establish a public dividend tied to AI-driven economic growth — giving working Californians a real stake in the technology reshaping their lives.
02
Legal Liability
Right now, developers of frontier AI systems bear almost no legal accountability for harms their systems cause. The Legislature should enact strict liability standards — ensuring that Californians harmed by AI have real, enforceable recourse.
03
Democratic Oversight
No corporation and no government should control AI alone. The Legislature should create an independent oversight body with authority to audit AI systems, set safety standards, and mandate civil society participation in governance.
The Resolution

The model text below may be adopted by the Berkeley City Council and transmitted to the California State Legislature.

Draft · 2026

A Resolution of the City Council of Berkeley, California,
Urging the California State Legislature to Enact
Comprehensive Artificial Intelligence Accountability Legislation

WHEREAS, artificial intelligence systems are rapidly transforming the economy, labor markets, and public life in California and across the nation; and

WHEREAS, the City of Berkeley has a long tradition of civic leadership on issues of technology, civil rights, and social justice, and has consistently acted to protect the well-being of its residents and set precedent for the state; and

WHEREAS, AI systems developed and deployed without adequate accountability mechanisms pose risks of economic displacement, discriminatory outcomes, and dangerous concentrations of power; and

WHEREAS, leading economists estimate that AI automation may expose hundreds of millions of jobs globally to displacement, with disproportionate impacts on working-class and middle-income Californians; and

WHEREAS, the benefits of AI-driven productivity growth are currently accruing primarily to technology companies and their shareholders, rather than to the workers and communities whose labor and data have contributed to these systems; and

WHEREAS, California is home to many of the world's leading AI research institutions, including the University of California, Berkeley, giving our state a special responsibility and a unique opportunity to lead on AI governance; and

WHEREAS, the California State Legislature has the authority and the obligation to protect Californians from the harms of unaccountable AI systems while ensuring the gains of this technology are shared broadly; now, therefore, be it

RESOLVED, That the City Council of the City of Berkeley urges the California State Legislature to enact legislation establishing a public AI dividend that distributes a meaningful share of AI-driven productivity gains to all Californians, with priority given to workers and communities experiencing labor market displacement as a result of AI adoption; and be it further

RESOLVED, That the City Council urges the California State Legislature to enact strict liability standards for developers and deployers of frontier AI systems, ensuring that individuals and communities harmed by such systems have meaningful and enforceable legal recourse; and be it further

RESOLVED, That the City Council urges the California State Legislature to establish an independent AI oversight body with authority to audit AI systems, set safety and transparency standards, and ensure meaningful civil society representation in AI governance decisions affecting Californians; and be it further

RESOLVED, That the City Clerk is directed to transmit a certified copy of this resolution to the Governor of California, the President pro Tempore of the California State Senate, the Speaker of the California State Assembly, and the Mayor and City Council of each city in Alameda County.
